Where The Journey Began...

At an ealry age as an adolesecent, my father taught me not to view physical work as a chore, but to embrace it as exercise, which vital to human health. He would always tell me this whenever I didn't want to do phyical chores. So as I began to get older, I began to challenge myself in fun ways doing chores and this helped me challenge myself in different ways. My family owned a convenience store and one way I would challenge myself, would be to see how many heavy grocery boxes I could lift in as little of time as possible without getting tired and doing it in the correct way my was taught. My father would say "make sure you're lifting with your legs and not you back son!". As the years progressed and I got older, I then began enjoying physical labor, and it was no longer "as a pain in the rear" as it was earlier. My journey into fitness was just beginning to take shape.

The Adventure

My passion for fitness began early on when I was in my early 20's. In my early 20's I got my first gym membership at Bally's Total Fitness. I would always see commercials on television and how nice the equipement was and how everyone in the commercial looked really cool exercising, and I began to envision myself in the same light as those who were on the TV commercials. The gym was located in the Clayton area on the street called Forsyth. Fitness was so important to me, I would essentially put the rest of my life on hold, if I had't exercised for that day. I can remember, making friends of mine wait untill AFTER I'd workout, before we would go out and have a night on the town. Friends and I thinking back to that period of time, makes us laugh till this very day. I religously exercised and it began to rub off on my close friends to the point, they all joined BTF and got more into fitness as well.

At The Cross Road

The more I got into fitness, the more my body began to change and take shape into a more atheletic pyhsique, the more I had a healhty addiction to exercise and over the years began to incorporate nutrition also into what I was doing. Fast forward from my 20's to my 30' and my journey brought me to a whole new world and level of fitness, Natural Body Building. I remembered someone I knew from my days working out at Bally's, who was employed there as one of the lead personal trainers. I'd heard that he'd opened up his own fitness studio and at this time I'd wanted to take my fitness to the next level and become more toned. So I visited his studio to sit with him and have him customize a nutritional plan to help me shed a few extra pounds. Hoever, after we talked, before I knew it I'd signed up to have him train me to compete in my very first natural body building competition.

Test Of Self

Needless to say, I really didn't fully understand the magnitude of what I had just signed up to do. I THOUGHT I would simply have to change the way I was eating, and simply continue to work out the way I became accustomed to working. Boy was I wrong! Just imagine, from a nutritional standpoint, taking your current lifestyle of eating, and completely stripping away everything you love to eat, for a total of 3 to to 4 months. That means no sugar, no alcohol (if you enjoy that), non of your favorite pizza, ice cream, everything that you've become to enjoy. As far as exercising, imagine doing the most strenious and hardest exercise you've ever done, and multiply that times 100! This by far was the most grueling challenge I've taken in my entire life, physically and most of all mentally! But nothing in life worth while costs you very little. The cost was very high physically and mentally pushing me past limits I thought I could never achieve and much more! However, all of the anguish and pain I went through in my training, definitely paid off in the end!

Victory

My very first body building competiton, in the novice category, I won 1st place in my height class. The joy I felt in winning 1st place, for my very FIRST show, was undescribable. All of my hard work paind off! All of the days and nights where I questioned myself "why am I doing this", "why am I putting myself through so much pain" was realized when I was handed that 1st place trophy! I went on to compete in two more shows in what's called the "Open Category" in which competitors compete for the opportunity to gain a "Pro Card" which allows a competitor to compete in Natural Body Building on a professional level for all kinds of different prizes, prize monies, and national recognition. So in my second competition, in the open category, I won 3rd place, which was a very tough competition. For my third competition, competing again in the open category, I won 5th place, again another tough category. I'm not one to make excuses, however in this particular competition, my body was in the best shape its every been amongst the shows I competed in, however I used a tanning solution without testing it before hand. This made my muscles look soft or "washed out", as it's called in the BB community, so I beleive, had I not done that, I could have taken a higher placing than what I was awarded. But, as they say Proper Preperation Prevets Poor Performance (the 4 P's)!
